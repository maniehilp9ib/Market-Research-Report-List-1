<p><p>Weakly acidic acrylic acid cation exchange resins are categorized into Type I and Type II based on their chemical structure and functionality. Type I resins contain carboxylic acid groups that facilitate cation exchange at lower pH levels, making them effective in removing divalent and trivalent ions. Type II resins, on the other hand, have a higher capacity for cation exchange under varying pH conditions, offering better performance in diverse applications. Both types are crucial for water treatment, food processing, and chemical industries.</p></p>
